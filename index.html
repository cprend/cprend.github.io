Channing Prend

I am a senior at Columbia University studying Earth Science and Math. I use a combination of theory, observations, and remote sensing to investigate large-scale ocean circulation, air-sea interactions, and the role of the ocean in climate. I am particularly interested in Southern Ocean dynamics, including: transport of heat and freshwater by the Antarctic Circumpolar Current, the impact of sea ice variability on the strength of the overturning circulation, and the effect of air-sea fluxes on Antarctic Bottom Water formation in the marginal seas. This fall I will begin my PhD in physical oceanography at Scripps Institution of Oceanography.

My pursuit of science has always been strongly rooted in a desire to impact society and better our understanding of the changing planet. Therefore, I am also very passionate about STEM outreach and communicating climate science to the general public. To this end, I interned at the NYC Mayor's Office of Sustainability helping design public education campaigns on issues such as environmentally-friendly city services and green infrastructure.
