Channing Prend

I am a PhD student studying physical oceanography at Scripps Institution of Oceanography. I use a combination of numerical models, observations, and remote sensing to investigate the role of the ocean in climate. I am particularly interested in Southern Ocean dynamics, including: air-sea-ice interactions, transport of heat and nutrients via the meridional overturning circulation, and physical controls on biogeochemistry.

My pursuit of science has always been strongly rooted in a desire to impact society. Therefore, I am also very passionate about communicating climate science to the general public. As an undergraduate, I interned at the NYC Mayor's Office of Sustainability helping design public education campaigns on issues related to energy efficiency, and served as content manager for a popular science blog called the Columbia Science Review. I still participate in frequent outreach events related to ocean science education or increasing LGBT visibility in STEM fields.
